Turnstile announce UK dates for Autumn Tour

Following a string of festival appearances and a pretty unforgettable Glasto set, Turnstile have confirmed they’ll be back in the UK this November for a headline run. It’s loud, it’s fast, and it’s going to be very sweaty.

They’ll be hitting three major UK cities with support from High Vis and The Garden on select dates. If you’ve been waiting to see the Baltimore band in a room full of people losing their minds, this is your shot.

Here’s where and when it’s going down:

  • 2 Nov - Glasgow, O2 Academy

  • 3 Nov - Manchester, Depot Mayfield

  • 5 Nov - London, Alexandra Palace

  • 31 Oct - Dublin, National Stadium

No word yet on surprise guests, but with this line-up already locked, it’s looking like one of those tours people talk about for a while.

Turnstile came up through Baltimore’s hardcore scene and have since grown into one of the most exciting live bands around. Led by frontman Brendan Yates, with Franz Lyons, Daniel Fang, Pat McCrory, and new member Meg Mills, they’ve built a sound that pulls from punk, rock, and beyond—without ever feeling forced.

Their latest album Never Enough dropped in June and lands with a sense of focus and ease they’ve grown into over time. Tracks like “Birds” and “Seein’ Stars” are already slotting into their sets, adding new weight without losing the energy they’re known for. Whether it’s a packed theatre or a huge room like Ally Pally, Turnstile still manages to make the whole place move as one.

Pre-sale kicks off 10am on Tuesday 2 July, with general sale opening 10am Thursday 4 July. Set a reminder. Sell-outs feel likely.
